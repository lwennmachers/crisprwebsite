Level 2: CRISPR Applications in Cystic Fibrosis

How Can CRISPR Treat Cystic Fibrosis?

Cystic Fibrosis (CF) is a genetic disorder caused by mutations in the CFTR gene, leading to thick mucus in the lungs and other organs. Researchers are exploring CRISPR gene editing to correct these genetic mutations at the DNA level. By replacing faulty CFTR sections with healthy ones, CRISPR could potentially provide a long-term solution to the root cause of CF. When successful, this process can lead to less thick mucus, fewer lung infections, and improved overall lung function, giving patients a significantly better quality of life.

Want to learn more? Check out the Cystic Fibrosis Foundation for detailed information on CF and current research.
