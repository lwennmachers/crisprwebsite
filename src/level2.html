How Can CRISPR Treat Cystic Fibrosis?

Cystic fibrosis (CF) is a genetic disorder caused by mutations in the CFTR gene, leading to thick mucus in the lungs, pancreas, and other organs. CRISPR can potentially correct these mutations at the DNA level by using the Cas9 protein to cut out faulty sections and replace them with healthy onesâ€”offering a long-term solution to the root cause of CF.
