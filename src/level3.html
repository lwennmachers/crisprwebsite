Level 3: Ethical Dilemmas of CRISPR

Understanding the Ethical Concerns

CRISPR technology holds incredible promise for treating genetic diseases, but it also introduces serious ethical questions. Off-target edits can accidentally alter unrelated genes, leading to unforeseen health problems. Mosaicism can occur, where not all cells are edited uniformly, potentially resulting in unpredictable outcomes. Moreover, changes made to germline cells (like human embryos) may be passed on to future generations, amplifying mistakes or unintended consequences.

Other ethical debates include whether scientists should have free rein to create genetically modified organisms, “designer babies,” and the potential impact on socioeconomic inequalities if such treatments are not equitably distributed. It is essential to weigh these risks and concerns alongside CRISPR’s benefits, to ensure responsible use of gene-editing technology.
