Level 1: How CRISPR Works

Welcome to Level 1

CRISPR-Cas9 is a revolutionary tool that allows scientists to make precise edits to DNA. At its core, CRISPR relies on a guide RNA (gRNA) to target specific DNA sequences, and the Cas9 protein to cut the DNA at the desired location. This mechanism enables genetic engineering for a variety of applications, from correcting mutations to advancing biological research.

The CRISPR system interacts with key DNA features to execute its functionality:

  • Guide RNA (gRNA): Guides Cas9 to the target DNA sequence.
  • Cas9 Protein: Molecular scissors to make precise cuts in DNA.
  • Protospacer Adjacent Motif (PAM): A recognition site near the target DNA.
